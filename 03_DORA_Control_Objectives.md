# DORA Control Objectives Model

## Purpose

This document outlines a simplified mapping between DORA regulatory areas and operational control objectives.

The model aims to support financial institutions in translating regulatory requirements into practical governance controls.

---

## Control Objective Mapping

| DORA Area | Control Objective | Example Evidence |
|----------|------------------|----------------|
| ICT Governance | Ensure oversight of ICT risk at management level | ICT governance framework |
| ICT Risk Management | Identify and assess ICT risks | ICT risk register |
| Incident Management | Detect and manage ICT incidents | Incident response procedures |
| Third-Party Risk | Monitor ICT outsourcing dependencies | Vendor risk assessments |
| Operational Resilience | Ensure continuity of critical services | Business continuity testing reports |

---

## Supervisory Perspective

Supervisory authorities typically expect institutions to demonstrate:

- documented ICT governance arrangements
- clear risk ownership structures
- effective incident management processes
- monitoring of ICT service providers

- resilience testing frameworks.

## Example Control Implementation

Control Area: ICT Incident Management

Control Objective:
Ensure that ICT-related incidents are identified, classified and reported in a timely manner to maintain operational resilience.

Control Description:
All ICT incidents must be recorded in the incident management system. Incidents are classified based on severity levels and escalated according to the internal escalation matrix.

Control Owner:
Head of IT Operations

Control Frequency:
Continuous monitoring

Evidence:
- Incident register
- Incident reports
- Escalation records
- Post-incident analysis documentation

Key Risk Indicator (KRI):
Number of high-severity ICT incidents per quarter.
