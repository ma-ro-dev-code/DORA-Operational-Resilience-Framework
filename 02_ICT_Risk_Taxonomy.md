# ICT Risk Taxonomy under DORA

## Objective

This document provides a simplified ICT risk taxonomy aligned with the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA).

The taxonomy helps financial institutions structure the identification and classification of ICT-related risks affecting operational resilience.

---

## Key ICT Risk Categories

### Infrastructure Risk

Risks arising from failures in physical or virtual infrastructure.

Examples:

- cloud service outages
- network failures
- data centre disruptions
- hardware malfunctions.

Potential impact:

- system downtime
- interruption of portfolio management activities
- transaction processing delays.

---

### Cybersecurity Risk

Risks associated with malicious attacks targeting ICT systems.

Examples:

- ransomware attacks
- phishing campaigns
- malware infections
- unauthorized system access.

Potential impact:

- data breaches
- service disruption
- reputational damage.

---

### Data Integrity Risk

Risks affecting the accuracy, completeness or availability of data.

Examples:

- corrupted data
- loss of transaction records
- incorrect data processing.

Potential impact:

- incorrect financial calculations
- inaccurate client reporting
- regulatory reporting errors.

---

### Application Risk

Risks related to failures or deficiencies in software systems.

Examples:

- software bugs
- failed system updates
- configuration errors.

Potential impact:

- transaction failures
- operational delays
- incorrect system outputs.

---

### Third-Party ICT Risk

Risks resulting from reliance on external technology providers.

Examples:

- cloud provider outage
- vendor cybersecurity incidents
- failure of SaaS platforms.

Potential impact:

- operational disruption
- loss of critical services
- dependency risk.
