# DORA Operational Resilience Framework – Structural Overview

## Purpose

This document outlines the conceptual structure of the DORA operational resilience framework presented in this repository.

The objective is to translate regulatory requirements under the EU Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) into a structured governance and risk management model applicable to financial institutions.

The framework focuses on practical implementation from the perspective of regulated financial entities such as asset managers and investment firms.

---

## Core Pillars of the Framework

The framework is structured around five core domains derived from DORA requirements.

### 1. ICT Governance

ICT governance ensures that the management body maintains effective oversight over ICT systems and associated risks.

Key elements include:

- board-level ICT risk oversight
- ICT strategy alignment with business objectives
- clear allocation of ICT responsibilities
- integration of ICT risk into enterprise risk management.

---

### 2. ICT Risk Management

ICT risk management focuses on identifying, assessing and mitigating risks affecting digital systems and infrastructure.

Typical ICT risks include:

- infrastructure failures
- cybersecurity threats
- software and application errors
- data integrity issues.

---

### 3. ICT Incident Management

Financial institutions must establish processes to detect, classify and manage ICT-related incidents.

Key elements include:

- incident detection mechanisms
- classification of incident severity
- escalation procedures
- regulatory reporting obligations.

---

### 4. Third-Party ICT Risk Management

Financial institutions increasingly rely on external ICT service providers.

DORA therefore requires institutions to manage risks arising from outsourcing and technology providers.

Key elements include:

- vendor risk assessments
- outsourcing governance
- monitoring of critical ICT providers
- exit and contingency planning.

---

### 5. Operational Resilience Testing

Institutions must regularly test their ability to withstand ICT disruptions.

Testing may include:

- business continuity testing
- disaster recovery exercises
- scenario-based resilience testing
- threat-led penetration testing.

---

## Implementation Approach

The framework presented in this repository follows a structured interpretation methodology:

1. Regulatory requirement extraction  
2. Risk objective mapping  
3. Control principle formulation  
4. Evidence expectation modelling  
5. Supervisory assessment simulation.

---

## Intended Use

The analytical artefacts developed in this repository may support:

- regulatory impact analysis
- internal control design
- ICT risk governance frameworks
- supervisory readiness assessments.
